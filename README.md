# Open-Source-Produce-Sanitation-Tunnel
An open-access blueprint for non-thermal, 360-degree sub-sonic sanitation of delicate produce during Cyclospora outbreaks.

System OverviewThe PFPT is an inline processing system engineered for industrial fruit and vegetable packaging houses. It is designed to eradicate tough, shell-bound protozoan pathogens (such as Cyclospora cayetanensis) from delicate, raw produce (berries and leafy greens) without generating heat or altering food cellular structures.The system achieves a 99.9% pathogen reduction rate by combining four mechanical, fluid, and optical milestones into a continuous automated processing line.2. 
Granular Technical SpecificationsStage 1: The Shallow-Bed Ultrasonic CascadeThe Problem: Standard ultrasonic baths feature deep water columns that cause high acoustic attenuation (loss of energy) before the sound waves reach the food.The Open Solution: A continuous-flow, slightly inclined stainless steel flume bed. The water layer is strictly limited to a shallow depth of 0.5 inches to 1.5 inches (1.27 cm to 3.8 cm).Mechanical Spec: The underside of the 316L marine-grade stainless steel bed is lined with an array of industrial 40 kHz piezoelectric sandwich transducers.The Physics: The shallow water layer allows the 40 kHz frequency to create localized, high-density microscopic cavitation bubbles. When these bubbles implode against the produce, they generate micro-jets that cleanly tear the parasite's protein-based "glue" away from the plant hairs and deep crevices, suspending the live pathogen in the fluid layer without bursting the plant’s fragile cells.
Stage 2: The Multi-Beam Diffractive Optics & 360° Photon CavityThe Problem: Short-wave ultraviolet light (UV-C) is strictly line-of-sight. Parasites hide in shadows. Furthermore, standard silver mirrors, aluminum, and steel absorb UV-C, converting it to heat and wasting energy.The Open Solution: High-output 254 nm UV-C LED arrays are mounted to water-cooled aluminum heat sinks on the tunnel ceiling.Optical Spec: The light path is focused through a Cylindrical Plano-Convex Lens made of Synthetic Fused Silica (Quartz Glass), which compresses the scattered light into a hyper-concentrated linear "light knife" delivering a local irradiance of 40 mJ/cm².The Splitter: Directly below the lens, the light passes through a nanoscale-etched Fused-Silica Diffractive Optical Element (DOE). The DOE splits the single master beam into a 5-way geometric "fan array" of equal-intensity light lines targeting the top, left, right, and bottom angles of the passing food stream simultaneously.The Cavity Walls: The entire interior of the tunnel hood and the shallow water tray is lined with Expanded PTFE (Polytetrafluoroethylene) sheet polymer. Because PTFE offers 99% diffuse reflectivity for UV-C wavelengths, it transforms the tunnel interior into a photon mirror maze. Light bounces continuously from every angle, penetrating under leaves and into berry hollows, completely erasing shadow zones and recycling missed energy.
Stage 3: Coordinated Pulse-and-Flush Cycles (Continuous Conveyor)The Problem: Continuous ultrasonic exposure causes mechanical tissue fatigue, turning lettuce limp. Additionally, dislodged dirt and plant sap create water turbidity (cloudiness), which scatters and neutralizes UV-C light.The Open Solution: The food travels down the flume on an open-wire stainless steel or quartz-fiber mesh conveyor belt.Automation Loop: The system utilizes a automated PLC loop: 7 seconds of sub-sonic pulsing, followed immediately by a 4-second high-velocity water flush via overhead micro-nozzles using clean, pre-filtered water.The Physics: The 4-second rest interval allows the plant tissue to recover its structural crispness. The high-velocity flush continuously clears away dirty, turbid water, keeping the fluid layer perfectly translucent so the UV-C light knives can pass through unhindered. The physical turbulence of the flush tumbles and rotates the loose berries and leaves, ensuring a brand-new surface area faces the light field with every subsequent pulse.
Stage 4: Closed-Loop Drain Bio-SecurityThe Problem: Flushing millions of dislodged, live parasites down the factory drain risks contaminating municipal wastewater systems, which can cycle back into public rivers and re-contaminate agricultural irrigation ditches downstream.The Open Solution: The factory effluent is channeled into a restricted 0.2 mm thin-film spiral glass tube.Mechanical Spec: The paper-thin water layer is continuously exposed to an isolated, high-power secondary UV-C LED block. Because the fluid is forced into a thin film, the UV-C light easily pierces the water, cross-linking the DNA of 100% of outgoing pathogens and permanently neutralizing them before the water enters the public plumbing grid.3. 
Materials & Sourcing Reference for EngineersTo build a DIY or factory-scale prototype of this system, developers should source the following component categories:Chassis & Bed: 316L Stainless Steel (to resist ultrasonic pitting and erosion).Optical Lenses: Synthetic Fused Silica / Quartz Optics (do not use standard glass or acrylic; they block UV-C).Reflective Lining: Expanded PTFE (Spectralon or Gore Diffuse Reflector Material equivalents) vacuum-sealed or mechanically fastened to the interior walls.Drainage Control: High-flow, smooth-bore industrial 24V Solenoid Pinch Valves (prevents internal catch-points where parasites can collect).
